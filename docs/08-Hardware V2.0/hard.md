## Version 2:
My module worked exactly as I needed it to, so the changes I'd make to the design are fairly minimal. 

The biggest change would be uncluttering the area on the PCB for voltage regulation, although it worked fine, it's a disorganized bunch of components, traces, and vias which makes it difficult to tell what's doing what. Additionally making the area more organized would make it easier to test with a multimeter, easier to solder, and overall more aesthetically pleasing.

Another small issue that got in the way a bit was the placement of the fuse, although it didn't block the jumpers it was placed next to, it's quite close and it makes it difficult to add or remove connectors. This was consistently frustrating and could easily be fixed by moving the fuse just a few millimeters away.

In addition to the list of changes above, below is a list of small changes. They have little effect on function, but may help the board look more put together, and make it easier to understand the component's functions:
- Align the upstream and downstream headers, they are very close to aligned, but for some reason I did not align them.
- Cut out a hole under the fuse holder, this is intended to be there so you can see through the fuse to check if it's broken.
- Move the camera a few millimeters forward, to align itself with the edge of the board.
- Move the micro SD holder a few millimeters forward, to align itself with the edge of the board.
- Move the LEDs a few millimeters to the left to align them with other components.
- Make all the test points a single hole rather than 2.
- Label the upstream and downstream headers on the silkscreen.
- Move the barrel jack back a few millimeters, to align itself with the edge of the board.
